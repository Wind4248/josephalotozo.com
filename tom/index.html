FAQ

Common Questions

What type of photography does Tom shoot?

Primarily landscape, travel, and portrait — shot almost entirely on analog film. Based in Columbus, Ohio.

What cameras does Tom shoot with?

A Nikon FE, a Holga, and Polaroid — ranging from precision 35mm to lo-fi medium format to instant film. Each camera brings a different feel to the work.

What film stocks does Tom use?

Ilford HP5 for black and white, and Kodak Portra, Ektar, and Gold for color — each chosen for how it handles light, grain, and shadow in a given scene.

Where does Tom develop his film?

Tom sends his film to Midwest Photo in Columbus, Ohio for development, then scans the negatives himself for full control over the final image.

Does Tom sell prints?

Yes — fine art prints are made to order from film originals. Email lotozot@gmail.com to inquire about a specific image.

Is Tom available for portrait or commercial work?

Tom's current focus is personal fine art — landscape, travel, and portrait work for the love of the medium. Print inquiries are always welcome.

How long has Tom been shooting film?

Five years — long enough to have strong opinions about film stocks, short enough to still be surprised by what comes back from the lab.

How do I get in touch?

Email lotozot@gmail.com or find Tom on Instagram at @tibetanplatoz.
