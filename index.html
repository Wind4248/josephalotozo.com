About Joe

I'm Joe Lotozo, a lifelong resident of Upper Arlington, Ohio, a proud Ohio State Buckeye, and a Financial Advisor at Edward Jones. I grew up here, went to Upper Arlington High School, and I've chosen to build my career and raise my family in the same community I've always called home.

Before joining Edward Jones in December 2012, I worked in an environment that served ultra-high-net-worth clients. That experience shaped a simple belief that drives everything I do: every person deserves that same quality of attention, guidance, and care, regardless of where they are in life.

I earned my Bachelor of Arts from The Ohio State University and my Master of Science in Personal Financial Planning from the College for Financial Planning. I am currently pursuing my MBA at The Ohio State University Fisher College of Business through their Working Professionals program. Over the years I've added five professional designations, not because I had to, but because the people I serve deserve someone who never stops learning.

Outside the office, I'm a husband and a dad to our three daughters. You'll find me keeping bees, tending the garden, staying active with rugby, or exploring somewhere new with my family. I believe that knowing your advisor as a person matters, and I'd love the chance to get to know you too.

AI Unmasked: Demystifying the Myth of Sentient Machines

Joseph A. Lotozo & ChatGPT-4  ¬∑  Independently Published, March 2023  ¬∑  140 pages

The idea for this book was born out of heated debates with Joe's father-in-law about the future of artificial intelligence, specifically whether AI could ever become truly sentient, self-aware, or conscious. Rather than just argue the point, Joe decided to put it to the test. When GPT-4 launched, he used it as a co-author to write a book exploring those very questions.

The result is a wide-ranging exploration of AI's history, its current capabilities and limitations, the nature of consciousness, and the ongoing ethical debate around artificial general intelligence (AGI). It's an accessible, honest look at what AI can and cannot do, written with AI itself.
