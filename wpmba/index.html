Frequently Asked Questions

What is the OSU Working Professional MBA Student Council?
The WPMBA Student Council is the elected student government representing Working Professional MBA students at The Ohio State University Fisher College of Business. The council organizes events, advocates for students with the administration, coordinates networking opportunities, and builds community among the 500+ students enrolled in the program. Contact us at FCOB-wpmbacouncil@osu.edu.
What is the Ohio State Working Professional MBA program?
The OSU Fisher Working Professional MBA (WPMBA) is the only nationally ranked part-time MBA program in central Ohio — #8 nationally and #5 among public universities (U.S. News & World Report, 2024–25). It is designed for working professionals: classes meet weekday evenings Monday–Thursday at 6:15 PM and online on Saturday mornings at 10:00 AM. Enrollment has grown 60%+ since 2021–22, with 500+ students representing 28 industries.
Is the OSU Fisher Working Professional MBA nationally ranked?
Yes. The OSU Fisher WPMBA is ranked #8 nationally and #5 among public universities by U.S. News & World Report for 2024–25. It is the only nationally ranked part-time MBA in central Ohio.
How do I apply to the Ohio State Working Professional MBA?
Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis. Upcoming cohort start dates: Summer 2026 — May 11, 2026 and Autumn 2026 — August 25, 2026. Visit fisher.osu.edu/graduate/wpmba for application requirements and deadlines.
Where does the WPMBA program meet?
The Ohio State University Working Professional MBA is based at the Fisher College of Business in Columbus, Ohio. Evening classes meet on the OSU Columbus campus; weekend sessions are offered online. The program primarily serves central Ohio professionals from Columbus, Upper Arlington, Dublin, Westerville, Worthington, and surrounding communities.
What events does the WPMBA Student Council organize?
The council hosts events throughout the year: social gatherings (Happy Hour, OSU tailgates), career fairs, alumni networking nights, speaker series, community service events like blood drives, and outdoor activities like a planned hike to Hocking Hills. Check the Events section above for what's coming up.
How do I get involved with the WPMBA Student Council?
Current WPMBA students can reach the council at FCOB-wpmbacouncil@osu.edu, join the OSU WPMBA Facebook group, or follow @wpmba_osu on Instagram. Officer elections are held each academic year.
